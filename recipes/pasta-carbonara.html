Quick Pasta Carbonara

Caution this is a made up recipe, intended for educational purposes!

Description

Carbonara is one of the most popular pasta dishes in Italy and the world. It is a rich,
indulgent dish that many people are surprised to find out only takes 30 minutes from start
to finish. The name carbonara comes from the Italian word for coal miner (carbonaro),
as it is said that this dish was eaten by coal miners during their lunch breaks. Made with
pancetta (or bacon) and plenty of Parmesan!!

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound pancetta or thick cut bacon, diced
  • 1 to 2 garlic cloves, minced, about 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • 3 to 4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or pecorino cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti (or bucatini or fettuccine)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
